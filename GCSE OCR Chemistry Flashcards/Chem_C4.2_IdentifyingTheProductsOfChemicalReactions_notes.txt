How do you test for carbon dioxide gas? | Bubble the gas through limewater - it will turn milky (cloudy)
How do you test for chlorine gas? | Put damp litmus paper into the gas - the paper is bleached and turns white
How do you test for hydrogen gas? | Use a burning splint at the open end of a test tube - creates a 'squeaky pop' sound
How do you test for oxygen gas? | Insert a glowing splint into the test tube - the splint relights in oxygen
What happens when you add a few drops of NaOH to calcium ions? | White precipitate forms
What happens when you add excess NaOH to calcium ions after the initial test? | No change (precipitate remains)
What happens when you add a few drops of NaOH to copper(II) ions? | Blue precipitate forms
What happens when you add a few drops of NaOH to iron(II) ions? | Green precipitate forms
What happens when you add a few drops of NaOH to iron(III) ions? | Orange-brown precipitate forms
What happens when you add a few drops of NaOH to zinc ions? | White precipitate forms
What happens when you add excess NaOH to zinc ions after the initial test? | Precipitate re-dissolves
