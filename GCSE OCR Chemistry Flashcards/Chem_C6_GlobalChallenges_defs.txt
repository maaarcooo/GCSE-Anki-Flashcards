What is acid rain? | Rain that is acidic due to gases like sulfur dioxide reacting with water vapour in clouds. Sulfur dioxide comes from burning fossil fuels with sulfur impurities.
What is addition polymerisation? (Higher Tier) | A reaction where many small monomers bond together to form a long chain polymer.
What are alcohols? (Higher Tier) | Organic compounds with the -OH functional group. First four: methanol, ethanol, propanol, butanol. Can be oxidised to carboxylic acids.
What are alloys? (Higher Tier) | Metal compounds made by combining two or more metals to give greater strength or corrosion resistance.
What are alkanes? (Higher Tier) | The most common hydrocarbons in crude oil. General formula: CnH2n+2. First four: methane, ethane, propane, butane.
What are alkenes? (Higher Tier) | Hydrocarbons with a double bond between carbons, making them unsaturated. General formula: CnH2n. First four: ethene, propene, butene, pentene.
What is bacterial extraction? | A metal extraction method using bacteria to break down low-grade ores, producing an acidic solution with metal ions.
What are carboxylic acids? (Higher Tier) | Organic compounds with -COOH functional group. First four: methanoic, ethanoic, propanoic, butanoic acid. Have typical acidic properties.
What is catalytic cracking? | Long-chain hydrocarbons are heated to gas, then passed over hot powdered aluminium oxide catalyst. Molecules split apart on catalyst surface.
What is a chemical cell? (Higher Tier) | A cell converting chemical energy to electrical energy. Made of two metal electrodes connected by electrolyte. Produces voltage until reactants used up.
What is chlorination? | Water treatment process where chlorine gas is injected to kill microbes.
What is climate change? | Change in global climate patterns largely caused by increased atmospheric carbon dioxide concentration.
What is combustion? (Higher Tier) | Burning a substance in oxygen. Energy transferred to surroundings as heat and light.
What is complete combustion? (Higher Tier) | Combustion in sufficient oxygen. Only products from hydrocarbon combustion are water and carbon dioxide.
What is condensation polymerisation? (Higher Tier) | Reactions where monomers join together and lose small molecules like water. Involves monomers with two functional groups.
What is the contact process? (Higher Tier) | Process to make sulfuric acid from sulfur and water. Uses vanadium(V) oxide catalyst at 450°C and 2 atm pressure.
What is corrosion? (Higher Tier) | Destruction of materials by chemical reactions with environmental substances. E.g., iron rusts with oxygen and water.
What is cracking? | Process breaking down larger hydrocarbons to produce smaller, more useful molecules. Can be catalytic or steam cracking.
What is crude oil? | Finite resource found in rocks. Remains of ancient biomass (mainly plankton) buried in mud. Mostly contains hydrocarbons.
What is desalination? | Process to remove salts from seawater to make it potable.
What is a displayed formula? | Formula showing all bonds between every atom in a compound.
What is DNA? (Higher Tier) | Molecule encoding genetic instructions. Two polymer chains made from four nucleotides in double helix form.
What is electrolysis? | Splitting ionic compounds using electricity. Used for metal extraction when metal is more reactive than carbon.
What is a ferrous metal? | A metal containing iron in its composition.
What is fertiliser? (Higher Tier) | Chemical added to soil to increase fertility for better crop growth. Contains nitrogen, potassium, and phosphorus compounds.
What is filtration? | Separation technique to separate insoluble solid from solution. Used in making water potable.
What is a finite resource? | A resource that will eventually run out.
What is fossil fuel? | Natural fuel like coal and gas, formed from remains of ancient living organisms.
What is fractional distillation? | Process separating liquid mixtures using different boiling points in a fractionating column.
What is a fuel cell? (Higher Tier) | Electrochemical cell continuously producing voltage when supplied with fuel and oxygen.
What is a functional group? (Higher Tier) | Group of atoms responsible for how a compound reacts. Same in all compounds of a homologous series.
What is the greenhouse effect? | Increase in Earth's atmospheric temperature due to greenhouse gases trapping infrared radiation.
What are greenhouse gases? | Atmospheric gases maintaining Earth's temperature to support life. Include water vapour, carbon dioxide, methane.
What is ground water? | Water collecting in rocks that trap it underground.
What is the Haber process? (Higher Tier) | Industrial process producing ammonia from nitrogen and hydrogen. Conditions: 450°C, 200 atm, iron catalyst.
What is a homologous series? (Higher Tier) | Series of compounds with same functional group and similar chemical properties.
What are hydrocarbons? | Molecules made only of hydrogen and carbon atoms.
What is a hydrogen-oxygen fuel cell? (Higher Tier) | Fuel cell using hydrogen and oxygen as reactants. Only product is water. Overall reaction: 2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O
What is incomplete combustion? (Higher Tier) | Combustion with insufficient oxygen. Can produce toxic carbon monoxide and carbon particulates.
What is life-cycle assessment? | Analysis of a product's environmental impact considering raw materials, manufacturing, packaging, transport, use, and disposal.
What is a monomer? (Higher Tier) | Small short-chain molecule that can join together to form long-chain polymers.
What are nucleotides? (Higher Tier) | The monomers that make up DNA.
What is ore? | Rock containing metal compounds in sufficient amounts to make extraction worthwhile.
What are particulates? | Particles causing global dimming and human health problems. Carbon particulates (soot) from incomplete combustion.
What is phytoextraction? | Metal extraction method using plants to absorb metal compounds through roots. Plants burned to remove compounds from ash.
What is polyamide? (Higher Tier) | Polymer category with amide functional group in main chain. Formed by condensation reaction between diamine and dicarboxylic acid.
What is polyester? (Higher Tier) | Polymer category with ester functional group in main chain. Formed by condensation reaction between diol and dicarboxylic acid.
What is a polymer? (Higher Tier) | Large long-chain molecule made of many small monomers joined by covalent bonds.
What is potable water? | Water that is safe for humans to drink.
What is the reactivity series? | Series arranging metals by reactivity order. Used to predict reaction products.
What is reduction with carbon? | Metal extraction process for metals less reactive than carbon. Metal oxide heated with carbon to reduce it to metallic element.
What is a repeating unit? (Higher Tier) | Part of polymer whose repetition would produce the complete polymer chain.
What is sacrificial protection? (Higher Tier) | Protecting iron/steel from corrosion using a more reactive metal (often zinc). Creates physical barrier to oxygen and water.
What is sedimentation? | Water treatment process removing solids. Suspended solids fall to bottom as sediment for easy removal.
What is steam cracking? | Long-chain hydrocarbons heated to gas, mixed with steam and heated to very high temperature to split into smaller molecules.
What is structural formula? (Higher Tier) | Formula showing atom arrangement in molecule but not all bonds. E.g., CH₃CH₂COCH₃
What is waste water? | Water from industrial, domestic, agricultural, and commercial activity. Requires treatment before being potable.
