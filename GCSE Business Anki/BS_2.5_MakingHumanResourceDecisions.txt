What is an organisational structure? | An outline of reporting relationships, roles, and responsibilities of employees in an organisation.
What is hierarchy in an organisation? | The levels of authority within an organisation, ranking positions from top to bottom.
What is chain of command? | The formal line of authority that flows down from top management to lower-level employees, defining who reports to whom.
What is span of control? | The number of employees that a manager or supervisor effectively manages.
What is a centralised structure? | When decision-making authority is concentrated at the top of the organisation, with senior management making most key decisions.
What is a decentralised structure? | When decision-making authority is distributed throughout the organisation, with lower-level employees having more decision-making power.
What are the key characteristics of tall/hierarchical structures? | Multiple levels of management, centralised decision-making, and a long chain of command.
What are the key characteristics of flat structures? | Fewer levels of management, decentralised decision-making, and a short chain of command.
Name two advantages of tall structures. | Clear hierarchy of authority/defined roles and promotion opportunities for career advancement.
Name two disadvantages of tall structures. | Communication barriers between levels and slow decision-making process.
Name two advantages of flat structures. | Faster decision-making and encourages creativity/innovation through autonomy.
Name two disadvantages of flat structures. | Role ambiguity/lack of clear hierarchy and limited opportunities for career advancement.
What are six characteristics of effective communication? | Accurate, clear/easily understood, complete, appropriate for the intended audience, via the right medium, and provides chance for feedback.
What are three barriers to effective communication? | Poor spelling/grammar/handwriting, language difficulties/speaking speed/accents, and use of jargon.
What does full-time employment typically mean? | Working more than 35 hours per week.
What is shift working? | Working set hours outside the normal nine-to-five pattern, usually on a rotation basis.
What is a zero hours contract? | A contract that doesn't guarantee hours of work but expects workers to be available when needed.
What is a freelance worker? | A self-employed contractor who works for various businesses on a job-by-job basis.
Name two benefits of remote working for businesses. | Reduced costs for workspace and lower absentee rates.
Name two benefits of remote working for employees. | Flexibility to start/finish work at preferred times and less time spent traveling.
What are the four main levels of job roles in a business hierarchy? | Directors, senior managers, supervisors/team leaders, and operatives/support staff.
What is a person specification? | A document detailing the essential and desirable characteristics of the person suitable for a job.
What four elements might a person specification include? | Qualifications, experience, skills, and personal characteristics/attributes.
What is a job description? | A document detailing the features of the job including duties, hours, location, responsibilities, and pay/conditions.
What is internal recruitment? | When a person who already works for a business is appointed to a job role through promotion or reassignment.
What is external recruitment? | When a business hires an employee from outside the business.
Name three benefits of internal recruitment. | Staff familiar with business culture, business aware of employee's strengths/weaknesses, and no induction training needed.
Name three benefits of external recruitment. | Higher number of applicants, highly motivated new recruits, and new ideas/experience brought in.
What is a recruitment agency? | An organization that carries out the advertising and recruitment process on behalf of a business.
What is the difference between training and development? | Training is teaching new skills while development is improving existing skills.
What are three benefits of a well-trained workforce? | More productive staff, staff feel valued, and more flexible/resilient staff.
What is formal training? | Structured training with specific objectives, often delivered outside the business by experts.
What is informal training? | Less structured training that takes place as needed, usually within the business by colleagues.
What is self-learning? | When workers study or practice skills without a teacher or trainer present.
What is induction training? | Training delivered to new workers shortly after joining a business.
What is ongoing training? | Training provided throughout a worker's career to improve productivity and keep skills updated.

Name three reasons why businesses train and develop employees. | Meeting legal requirements, keeping skills up-to-date, and improving motivation/retention.
What is motivation? | The inner desire or willingness that propels a person to take action and achieve a specific goal.
What is intrinsic motivation? | Motivation that comes from within a person (values, beliefs, etc.).
What is extrinsic motivation? | Motivation that comes from external factors (rewards or punishments).
How does motivation impact productivity? | Motivated employees are more productive and efficient, generating higher output and quality.
How does motivation impact reliability? | Motivated employees are more reliable, showing up on time, meeting deadlines, and taking fewer sick days.
How does motivation impact turnover rates? | Motivated employees are more likely to stay long-term, reducing costly recruitment and training.
What is remuneration? | The basic wage or salary a worker receives for their labor.
What is commission? | A percentage of sales revenue paid to workers who sell products or services.
What is a bonus? | An additional payment given to staff for achieving specific goals or exceeding performance expectations.
What are fringe benefits? | Additional benefits offered to employees such as company cars, private healthcare, or gym memberships.
What is autonomy as a non-financial incentive? | Giving staff authority and resources to make decisions without management approval.
What is job enrichment? | Adding more challenging or meaningful tasks to a job to increase motivation and engagement.
What is job rotation? | Moving staff between different roles to expose them to new challenges and experiences.
According to Herzberg's Two Factor Theory, is money a motivator? | No, money is not a motivator, but lack of it leads to dissatisfaction.
According to Maslow's Hierarchy of Needs, what are the first two needs that motivate people? | Physiological needs (food, water, clothing) and safety needs (employment, shelter).
