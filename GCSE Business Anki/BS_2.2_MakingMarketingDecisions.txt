What is the marketing mix? | A framework of 4Ps (product, price, place, and promotion) that businesses use to create and implement successful marketing strategies.
What is the product design mix? | The combination of three elements that make up a product's design: function, aesthetics, and cost.
What is the function element of the product design mix? | The product's intended purpose and specific tasks it's designed to perform - how well it meets the needs of users.
What is the aesthetics element of the product design mix? | The product's visual and sensory appeal, including form, shape, color, and texture.
What is the cost element of the product design mix? | The production cost that affects the selling price - a good design balances cost and value.
What are the five stages of the product life cycle? | Development, introduction, growth, maturity, and decline.
What happens during the development stage of the product life cycle? | The business focuses on designing the product, has negative cash flow due to R&D costs, and develops initial marketing strategies.
What happens during the introduction stage of the product life cycle? | The product is launched, sales growth is slow, cash flow is negative due to high promotion costs, and marketing focuses on creating awareness.
What happens during the growth stage of the product life cycle? | Sales increase rapidly, cash flow becomes positive, and marketing focuses on differentiation and building brand loyalty.
What happens during the maturity stage of the product life cycle? | Sales growth slows, cash flow is positive, and marketing focuses on maintaining market share and increasing profitability.
What happens during the decline stage of the product life cycle? | Sales begin to decline, cash flow turns negative, and marketing focuses on reducing costs or discontinuing the product.
What are extension strategies in the product life cycle? | Techniques used to extend a product's life beyond its natural cycle by boosting sales and maintaining profitability.
What are the three types of product-related extension strategies? | Product improvements, line extensions, and repositioning.
What are promotion-related extension strategies? | Changes to marketing including new advertising, price promotions, and sales promotions.
What is product differentiation? | An attempt by a business to distinguish its products from competitors by creating features that help it stand out.
What are the benefits of successful product differentiation? | Increased demand, increased brand loyalty, and ability to charge higher prices.
What are the two main pricing strategy options for businesses? | High profit margin with lower volume (price skimming) or lower profit margin with higher volume (penetration pricing).
What is the freemium pricing model? | A model where businesses benefit from both high profit margins and high sales volumes (e.g., Spotify).
How does the number of USPs affect pricing strategy? | Products with many USPs and high differentiation can command higher prices.
How does technology affect pricing strategy? | Online platforms have created new strategies like freemium, where the initial product is free but users pay for additional features.
How does competition level affect pricing strategy? | Highly competitive markets often require lower prices, while less competitive markets allow for higher prices.
How does brand strength affect pricing strategy? | Strong brands with loyal customers can command higher prices.
How does the product life cycle stage affect pricing strategy? | Prices may be low in introduction, increase during growth, and need to be lowered again during maturity.
How have online marketplaces changed pricing strategies? | Retailers now offer price matching policies to remain competitive as customers can easily compare prices.
What is the role of promotion in the marketing mix? | To generate customer awareness, interest, and desire for a product/service and differentiate from competitors.
What are the advantages of advertising as a promotional method? | It can reach large audiences, increase brand awareness, and create a specific brand image.
What are the disadvantages of advertising as a promotional method? | It can be expensive, hard to measure effectiveness, and many customers ignore ads.
What are the advantages of branding as a promotional method? | It establishes recognition, builds trust and credibility, and differentiates from competitors.
What are the advantages of special offers as a promotional method? | They can quickly boost sales, help clear stock, encourage impulse purchases, and target specific customer segments.
What are the disadvantages of special offers as a promotional method? | They can be expensive, attract deal-seeking customers who aren't loyal, and reduce sales of full-priced products.
What are the advantages of sponsorship as a promotional method? | It builds brand awareness and credibility, creates emotional connections, and can help enter new markets.
What are the disadvantages of sponsorship as a promotional method? | It can be expensive, may not directly drive sales, and risks negative publicity if the sponsored entity faces a scandal.
What is viral marketing? | A strategy where businesses create easily shareable online content at specific times to promote their products.
How do businesses use e-newsletters for promotion? | They build email lists to repeatedly promote products and build deeper connections with potential customers.
What does "place" refer to in the marketing mix? | Where customers purchase the businesses' products/services.
What are distribution channels? | The various intermediaries through which goods/services move from the business to the end customer.
What is a three-stage distribution channel? | When a product moves from producer to retailer to final customer.
What is a two-stage distribution channel? | When a product moves directly from manufacturer to end customer (e-tailing).
What is drop-shipping in e-commerce? | A system where businesses sell products without holding stock, shipping directly from producer to customer.
What is a third-party logistics provider (3PL)? | Companies like Amazon that provide businesses with infrastructure and marketplace to reach wider audiences without investing in their own distribution.
How do changes to the product affect the marketing mix? | Changes to a product likely require changes to all other elements (price, promotion, place) to align with the new product's value.
How do changes to the price affect the marketing mix? | Price changes affect perceived brand value and may require changes to promotion strategy.
How do changes to promotion affect the marketing mix? | Promotion strategy can affect price and distribution channels.
How do changes to place affect the marketing mix? | Distribution channels can affect price and promotion strategy.
What is competitive advantage? | Features of a business and its products that are perceived as superior to rivals by customers.
What makes a competitive advantage "distinctive" and "defensible"? | Distinctive means different from competitors; defensible means the business can prevent competitors from copying it.
What are some sources of competitive advantage? | Quality, delivery times, low price, reliability, ethical stance, and design.
What is an integrated marketing mix? | One that correctly combines each element in the best possible way to create a cohesive strategy that resonates with customers.
