What is unlimited liability? | When owners are fully responsible for all business debts and may have to use personal assets to pay these debts if the business fails.
What is limited liability? | When owners (shareholders) can only lose the original amount they invested in the business if it fails and are not responsible for additional business debts.
What are the advantages of a sole trader? | Easy and inexpensive to set up, owner has complete control, all profits belong to the owner, simple tax arrangements.
What are the disadvantages of a sole trader? | Unlimited liability, limited access to finance, limited skill set of the owner.
What are the advantages of a partnership? | Easy to set up, shared responsibilities and decision-making, more skills and knowledge available, increased access to finance.
What are the disadvantages of a partnership? | Unlimited liability, potential for disputes between partners, profits often shared equally regardless of contribution, difficult to transfer ownership.
What are the advantages of a private limited company (Ltd)? | Limited liability, access to greater finance, easier to transfer ownership, can have a professional image and reputation.
What are the disadvantages of a private limited company (Ltd)? | More expensive and time-consuming to set up, more complex legal requirements, annual financial reporting required, shareholders have little control.
What is franchising? | A business model where an individual (franchisee) buys the rights to operate under an established business model and branding in exchange for fees.
What are the advantages of owning a franchise? | Centralized advertising, training provided, supplies provided, exclusive location, support services.
What are the disadvantages of owning a franchise? | Overhead/startup cost, royalty costs, cost of supplies may be inflated, quality control management (franchise rights can be removed).
What is meant by proximity to the market? | The distance between the business location and its target customers - being close reduces transportation costs and increases accessibility.
What is meant by proximity to labor? | The availability of qualified and skilled workers in the area - businesses often locate where they can access the necessary workforce.
What is meant by proximity to materials? | The availability of raw materials and supplies needed for the business - helps minimize transportation costs.
How has the internet impacted business location decisions? | Businesses can now reach customers from anywhere, making physical location less important, especially for e-commerce businesses.
What are the 4Ps of the marketing mix? | Product, Price, Place, and Promotion.
Why is promotion an important element of the marketing mix? | It generates customer awareness, communicates value proposition, differentiates from competitors, and builds brand awareness and loyalty.
What is the product design mix? | The combination of function, aesthetics, and cost that make up a product's design.
How does price factor into the marketing mix? | Price helps position the brand in the market, maximize revenue and profitability, and enables the business to compete effectively.
What does "place" refer to in the marketing mix? | The physical location of the business and/or the distribution channel through which the product goes from business to customer.
How might businesses change their pricing in a competitive environment? | They may lower prices, offer temporary discounts or promotions to attract customers.
How might businesses change their product in a competitive environment? | They may differentiate their product by improving quality, functionality, or adding unique features to stand out.
How has new technology impacted pricing strategies? | Businesses can use data analytics to analyze competitors' pricing, reduce costs, and implement dynamic pricing based on supply and demand.
How has new technology impacted product development? | Businesses can easily gather customer feedback to create and refine products that better meet customer needs.
How has new technology impacted promotion? | Businesses can reach target audiences with highly targeted marketing and create two-way communication with customers.
How has new technology impacted place/distribution? | E-commerce allows businesses to reach customers in any location at any time and offer a wider selection of products.
What is a business plan? | A document produced at start-up providing forecasts about the business idea, objectives, target market, finances, marketing mix, and more.
What is the main aim of producing a business plan? | To reduce the risk associated with starting a new business and help owners raise finance.
How can a business plan help obtain finance? | It allows lenders and investors to explore the plan and make informed decisions about whether the business is credible and worth the financial risk.
What key elements does a business plan usually contain? | Aims and objectives, details of target market, financial projections, and an outline of the marketing mix.
Why should business owners keep their business plans up to date? | It helps focus business activities, identify needed resources, and provides justification to potential investors.
