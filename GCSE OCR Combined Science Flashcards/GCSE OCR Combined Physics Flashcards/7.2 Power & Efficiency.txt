What is wasted energy? | Energy that is not useful for the intended purpose and is dissipated to the surroundings.
What does "dissipated" mean? | Spread out to the surroundings.
What is useful energy? | Energy that is transferred from store to store and used for an intended purpose.
What are the three common forms of wasted energy transfers? | Heating, light, and sound.
How does friction cause energy waste? | It transfers energy from the kinetic store by heating to objects and surroundings.
How can friction be reduced in machines? | By lubricating the parts that rub together.
What is thermal conductivity? | The rate of energy transfer by conduction across a material.
Do materials with high thermal conductivity heat up faster or slower? | Faster.
What is fibreglass made of? | Woven material with glass fibres held together, with trapped air between fibres.
Why is fibreglass a good insulator? | The trapped air has low thermal conductivity.
What are two ways cavity wall insulation reduces heat loss? | It has lower thermal conductivity than wall material and increased thickness slows cooling rate.
What is efficiency? | The ratio of useful energy output from a system to its total energy input.
What is the formula for efficiency using energy? | Efficiency = useful output energy transfer / total input energy transfer
What is the formula for efficiency using power? | Efficiency = useful power output / total power input
Can efficiency be greater than 1 or 100%? | No, it is always between 0-1 (or 0-100%).
Does efficiency have units? | No, efficiency has no units.
How do you convert efficiency from decimal to percentage? | Multiply by 100.
What are four causes of energy waste in machines? (Higher Tier) | Friction, air resistance, electrical resistance, and noise.
How can bearings improve efficiency? (Higher Tier) | They prevent components from directly rubbing together, reducing friction.
How can electrical resistance be reduced? (Higher Tier) | Use components with lower resistance or reduce the current.
How can air resistance be reduced? (Higher Tier) | By streamlining the shapes of moving objects.
How does noise waste energy? (Higher Tier) | Sound waves cause air particles to vibrate, transferring energy by heating to surroundings.
How can noise be reduced in machinery? (Higher Tier) | Tighten loose parts to reduce vibration and lubricate parts.
What two factors does energy transferred by an appliance depend on? | How long it's switched on and its power rating.
What does the power rating of an appliance measure? | The energy transferred per unit time (per second).
A 2000 W kettle transfers how much energy per second? | 2000 J per second.
What three things are shown on an appliance power label? | Potential difference required, frequency of supply, and power rating in watts.
In an electric heater, what is the useful energy transfer? | From thermal store of heating element to thermal store of air in room by heating.
In an electric heater, what is the wasted energy transfer? | Energy dissipated to surroundings by radiation (light).
In an electric motor lifting a load, what is the useful energy transfer? | From kinetic store of motor to gravitational potential store of load.
In an electric motor, what causes wasted energy? | Friction causes temperature rise, transferring energy to thermal store of surroundings.
In a filament bulb, what is the useful energy transfer? | From thermal store of filament wire to surroundings by radiation as visible light.
In a filament bulb, what is the main wasted energy transfer? | Energy transferred by heating to surroundings (most energy is heat, not light).
In an electric kettle, what is the useful energy transfer? | From thermal store of heating element to thermal store of water by heating.
What are three wasted energy transfers in an electric kettle? | To kettle casing, to surroundings from casing, and to surroundings as water evaporates.
Name three appliances that use electric motors. | Vacuum cleaners, washing machines, and refrigerators.
What happens if you plug an appliance into a mains voltage much higher than stated? | It could fuse, set fire, or become damaged.
