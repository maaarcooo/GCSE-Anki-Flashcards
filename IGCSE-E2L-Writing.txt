What is the main purpose of an article? | Usually to inform or persuade the reader about something.
What type of argument should an article present when persuading? | A balanced argument showing both sides (e.g., advantages and disadvantages in different paragraphs).
What tone should an article use? | Usually semi-formal, depending on the topic.
How should an article engage readers? | By being interesting and engaging, using techniques like rhetorical questions.
What does an article need at the beginning? | A title.
How many paragraphs should an article typically have? | Usually 4 paragraphs – introduction, main body (2 paragraphs), and conclusion.
What word count is recommended for IGCSE article writing tasks? | About 120 to 160 words.
How many marks are available for an article in the exam? | Up to 6 marks for content and up to 9 marks for language (total: 15 marks).
What is the main purpose of an essay? | To present an argument or point of view with examples or reasons to support it.
What tone should an essay use? | Formal and neutral (not showing too much emotion).
What are the two possible approaches when writing an essay? | Single approach (presenting one side of an argument) or balanced approach (presenting both sides).
What should the introduction of an essay include? | A brief outline of the argument and a statement of your opinion.
What linking words are appropriate for essays? | Formal linking words like "firstly," "in conclusion," "to sum up," "in addition," "however," and "furthermore."
What is the structure of a balanced essay? | Introduction (outline argument and state opinion), body paragraph 1 (reasons for), body paragraph 2 (reasons against), conclusion (restate opinion).
Who is typically the intended audience for an IGCSE essay task? | Usually a teacher or an educated reader.
What register should be used in an essay? | Formal to neutral, avoiding language that is too idiomatic or colloquial.
What is the purpose of a review? | To give an opinion about something (e.g., film, book, restaurant, video game, website) and recommend it (or not) to readers.
What are the three main functions of a review? | To inform (provide factual information), analyze (give opinions on positive/negative points), and advise (make a final judgment and recommendations).
How should the tone of a review vary based on audience? | More informal for teenage audiences; more formal for adult audiences.
What kind of title should a review have? | A specific title that mentions what is being reviewed, not just "A review of..."
What must a review include at the end? | A recommendation.
How should a review be structured? | Usually 4 paragraphs – introduction, main body (2 paragraphs), and conclusion/recommendation.
What makes a good review? | It should be engaging, informative, and persuasive from beginning to end.
Can a review be entirely negative? | A review can be positive, negative, or a mix of both, as long as opinions are supported.
What is a report usually about? | A specific event, written for the person/people who organized it or asked you to attend.
What information should a report contain? | Factual information, opinions, details, explanations, and both positive and negative points.
What tone should a report use? | Formal.
What formatting features should a report include? | A title and subheadings (usually underlined) for each paragraph.
What must a report include at the end? | A recommendation or suggestion (usually underlined).
How should a report be structured? | Usually 4 paragraphs – introduction, main body (2 paragraphs), and conclusion/recommendation.
Who is typically the intended audience for a report? | The organizers or people who requested the report.
What is the difference between a report and a review? | A report is more factual and formal, focused on events and providing recommendations to organizers, while a review is more opinion-based about products/services with recommendations for potential users.
